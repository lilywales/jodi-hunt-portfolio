My Fathers' Daughter

My Fathers’ Daughter is a powerful and intimate memoir, in which Hannah-Azieb Pool takes us on her extraordinary journey of self-discovery back to her birth home - Eritrea. The goal for this cover was for it to feel vibrant, optimistic, truthful, intimate and vulnerable. The collage-style allowed me to be playful but also serious at the same time. Below is a sneak peek into my design and development process. Check out my feature on Spine Magazine for more.

Through research I got a feel of the sights, sounds, colours and textures of Eritrea. I was amazed by the beautifully worn Art Deco buildings in the cities of Asmara and Keren. I took inspiration from the rich colours, circular windows, curves, diamond patterns and geometric ornamentation that lace the streets of Eritrea.

Obscuring Hannah-Azieb’s face felt like a very fitting tool for representing this story of identity and duality playfully - her westernised identity being displaced in these various forms that pay homage to her long-lost Eritrean identity. Finding the diamond shape ubiquitous in Eritrean patterns, I chose to play with half the diamond to depict this duality of her identity.

I chose Typography that would portray the curves, lines and worn textures of the Art Deco buildings. The warm and vibrant colours I used to represent the warmth felt from Hannah-Azieb’s rich experience. This I juxtaposed with her black and white portrait to show the colour that was brought through this life changing experience of meeting her family and venturing to her birth home.
